## World system

**Thesis:** Every inhabitant is a villain shaped by survival in a dry, tactile, occult world. Shared finish creates one world; trait grammar creates family resemblance; combat role creates board-readable function; individual body, face, species and psychology prevent uniformity.

### Shared finish

- Favor matte, dry, worn and porous surfaces; reserve gloss for a specific wet, vitreous or precious material with narrative cause.
- Show accumulated survival through repair, depletion, contamination, asymmetry, scar, posture or altered anatomy rather than decorative grime alone.
- Preserve controlled color. Desaturation is a finish, not a ban; each chromatic choice must carry trait or psychological meaning.
- Read hostile intent in mass, negative space and posture before lore, facial anger or floating effects explain it.
- Use one dominant visual thesis per unit. Repetition of small accessories never substitutes for a distinct body plan.
- Keep all conscious characters villain-first, while varying evil through manipulation, ferality, dissociation, numb obedience, theatricality, obsession, hunger, shame and predatory calm.

### Permanent lessons from prior passes

- Vellum is the material-richness and anti-gloss veto, never a shared costume template.
- Creep proves that narrow, alien, dissociated horror can outperform ornate gothic decoration; do not give every monster the same demon anatomy.
- Paisley proves that strange color, pattern and fabric movement belong in the world when weathered and psychologically specific.
- Morwen defines the attractive-adult lane: dangerous, wounded, controlled, villainous and non-explicit; appeal must not collapse into one face or clothing aisle.
- The horror pass overused rib masks, hooks, halos, black cloth and generic demon anatomy; symbolic meaning must live in anatomy, posture or behavior before ornament.
- The women pass overused black leather, long coats, corsets, heels, leg slits, rear poses and orbiting clutter; every person needs an individual face, body, garment family and form of villainy.

### Trait-channel hierarchy

- Art-primary trait owns the dominant mass, negative space and garment/anatomy family.
- Secondary trait owns a separate surface channel: palette, material, pattern or magical movement.
- A third trait, when present, owns one restrained cost mark, anatomical corruption or secondary rhythm.
- Combat role owns stance, reach, weight distribution and the speed implied by the silhouette.
- Individual identity owns face, age, gender expression, body type, species, history and survival psychology.
- A finished unit should show two strong trait signals and at most one subtle signal; more usually becomes visual soup.

### Roster-wide prohibitions

- No shared black-cloak, black-leather or tarnished-trinket uniform across unrelated traits.
- No same-face, same-body, same-corset, same-heel, same-leg-slit or same-rear-pose convergence.
- No trait may depend entirely on a halo, horn, hook, rib mask, floating object or orbiting effect.
- No clean heroic fantasy, porcelain skin, latex shine, glossy gacha finish, mascot anatomy or generic Bloodborne imitation.
- No automatic full-black palette. Neutrals must leave room for trait-specific color, material and pattern.
- No supernatural power without a visible price borne by body, memory, identity, relationship or environment.

### Phase 1 exit tests

- All 22 live traits have one complete card with every required Phase 1 field.
- Every trait has a prop-free definition that remains recognizable without a named costume, object or production effect.
- Every palette, pattern, material and motion family is distinct enough to avoid one shared gothic costume lane.
- Blessed reads as disgrace, shame, ruined miracle and compulsory penance before it reads as angelic.
- Sanguine supports courtly, medical, feral, monastic and nonhuman vampyr lanes rather than one vampire stereotype.
- Kaleidoscope reads as weathered psychedelic perception fracture rather than glossy rainbow crystal.
- Combination rules preserve separate visual channels and cap visible trait signals at two strong plus one subtle.
- The compact atlas contains only abstract reference information; it is not new unit artwork or production asset creation.

## Trait cards

### Aegis

**Mechanics anchor:** Defensive bonuses with cast-based defensive stacking for members and allies.

**Core fantasy:** Protection as incarceration: safety granted by something that encloses, claims and gradually turns the protected body into part of the ward.

**Emotional tone:** Possessive vigilance, punitive care and the claustrophobic relief of surviving inside a prison.

**Silhouette verbs:** encircle, interlock, brace.

**Silhouette language:** Nested masses and sheltering overhangs create guarded negative space. The figure should appear to close around a threat or protected volume rather than pose as a noble defender.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Nested architectural planes that overlap without becoming conventional plate
- Ringed or chambered anatomy that creates protected interior voids
- Dense wrapped and quilted construction with deliberate closure lines
- Scar lattices and calcified growth that record every absorbed impact
- Membranous or shell-like nonhuman enclosures

**Palette roles:** dominant: salt gray `#AAA59B`; support: oxidized teal `#476C68`; accent: smoke violet `#61546B`; shadow: bruised blue `#3F5267`; cost: tarnished silver `#787B78`.

**Patterns:** concentric closures, threshold bands, stress-line lattices.

**Materials:** salted limestone, weathered stained glass, hammered dull metal, scar tissue, dense felted fiber.

**Magic behavior:** Defense moves inward. Thin planes, scars or membranes close in sequence; impacts redirect through visible paths and leave new fractures rather than blooming as a clean blue bubble.

**Supernatural cost:** Each act of protection reduces sensation, flexibility or personal boundary. The defender calcifies, hollows or becomes a permanent enclosure for damage that never fully leaves.

**Allowed variations:**

- ward-jailer
- possessive martyr
- mobile refuge
- parasite protector
- alien chamber-organism
- architectural beast

**Forbidden cliches:**

- royal paladin
- clean blue force bubble
- ordinary full plate
- one round defensive emblem
- benevolent guardian posture

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Aegis owns enclosing mass and protected negative space.
- As secondary, Aegis appears in closure seams, stress paths or a bodily sacrifice—not extra defensive equipment.
- With Bulwark, Aegis supplies enclosing protection while Bulwark supplies compression and release.
- With Blessed, protection must deepen humiliation or bodily cost rather than sanctify the figure.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: nested alcoves and closing shells
- Surface: salt efflorescence crossed by stained-glass stress lines
- Motion: successive planes folding toward an impact
- Cost mark: calcified joints and sensation-dead skin
- Visual pass: protected void inside closing mass
- Visual fail: clean shield badge

**Prop-free definition:** A possessive defense that survives by enclosing bodies and slowly turning them into the prison itself.

### Arcanist

**Mechanics anchor:** Spell power, kill stacks and high-tier amplification or double-cast behavior.

**Core fantasy:** Forbidden spellcraft as invasive language: knowledge that rewrites the caster while rewarding mastery with increasingly illegal power.

**Emotional tone:** Controlled obsession, intellectual trespass and the cold pride of understanding something that should not be legible.

**Silhouette verbs:** taper, inscribe, suspend.

**Silhouette language:** Long, precise tensions terminate in sharp interruptions. The body feels edited, annotated or held in an unnatural grammatical pause instead of reading as a generic robed caster.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Inscribed skin and tendon lines that behave like living syntax
- Severe clerical, laboratory, itinerant and courtly knowledge-worker constructions
- Asymmetrical folded planes resembling edited or erased passages
- Black-glass or scarred anatomical inserts
- Alien mathematical bodies organized around impossible ratios

**Palette roles:** dominant: black glass `#17171D`; support: ink blue `#25354D`; accent: smoke violet `#594762`; shadow: acid chalk `#C9C6A1`; cost: old vellum `#A88F6A`.

**Patterns:** palimpsest erasure, branching syntax, interrupted marginal grids.

**Materials:** obsidian, soot, absorbed ink, scorched skin, faded woven fiber.

**Magic behavior:** Marks appear in a single disciplined locus, crawl under a surface, then burn away or repeat out of sequence. Double casts should feel like a forbidden sentence correcting reality twice.

**Supernatural cost:** Language consumes language: speech, memory, names or parts of the body are overwritten. Mastery leaves burns, missing recollections and anatomy reorganized into notation.

**Allowed variations:**

- contract occultist
- hedge mathematician
- court heretic
- tattooed medium
- alien logician
- illiterate vessel of living syntax

**Forbidden cliches:**

- generic purple wizard
- floating page cloud
- star-covered academic robes
- glowing marks across every surface
- clean fantasy spell circle

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Arcanist owns precision, taper and the inscribed or edited body.
- As secondary, it owns one controlled notation system or cast echo while the primary trait keeps the silhouette.
- With Scholar, Arcanist is transgressive application while Scholar is accumulated knowledge and bodily storage.
- With Overload, syntax should break cadence and misfire rather than simply glow brighter.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: tensioned verticals interrupted like edited sentences
- Surface: palimpsest ink over smoke-dark glass
- Motion: marks writing inward, burning away, then repeating
- Cost mark: lost speech and anatomy reorganized as notation
- Visual pass: one precise locus of illegal notation
- Visual fail: purple wizard stars

**Prop-free definition:** Illegal knowledge becomes living syntax that sharpens power while rewriting memory, language and flesh.

### Blessed

**Mechanics anchor:** Increased healing and protection, with excess restoration becoming further protection at higher tiers.

**Core fantasy:** Divine favor experienced as disgrace: fallen angels, failed saints, shame, ruined miracles and compulsory penance.

**Emotional tone:** Public humiliation, exhausted beauty, self-loathing, bitter obedience and cruelty born from being forced to perform mercy.

**Silhouette verbs:** bow, molt, burden.

**Silhouette language:** The body folds inward, drags or bears an unwanted sacred weight. Even a large or beautiful form must read as disgraced, watched and compelled before it reads as celestial.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Penitential wrapping, scorched ceremonial cloth and old medical fiber
- Molting, vestigial or inward-folded avian anatomy
- Ex-voto growths, stigmatic tissue and cracked gilded skin
- Failed-saint bodies marked by repeated restoration
- Nonhuman organisms forced to exude protective secretions

**Palette roles:** dominant: corpse ivory `#C8BFAE`; support: dirty wax `#B8A274`; accent: tarnished gold `#8B7043`; shadow: bruised lavender `#776476`; cost: soot blood `#4A2C2C`.

**Patterns:** broken radiance, penance tally scars, molt and burn gradients.

**Materials:** dirty candle wax, cracked gilding, old medical cloth, ash, scarred skin.

**Magic behavior:** A miracle should arrive reluctantly and transfer consequence. Healing exposes old wounds, draws injury into the source or builds tense scar-like protection instead of clean radiant spectacle.

**Supernatural cost:** Every miracle deepens visible shame: wounds migrate, tissue calcifies, sacred growths fail, beauty decays or the body is forced to display the suffering it removed from others.

**Allowed variations:**

- disgraced celestial
- failed saint
- involuntary healer
- hypocritical holy tyrant
- living evidence of a ruined miracle
- nonhuman penance organism
- bitter martyr

**Forbidden cliches:**

- clean holy hero
- radiant paladin
- decorative black-wing demon
- generic pretty sad angel
- lingerie or fake sacred protection
- perfect floating halo
- gold used without psychological consequence

**Combination rules:**

- Blessed always owns shame, posture, facial condition or visible miracle cost even when secondary.
- Celestial anatomy is optional; disgrace and compulsory mercy are mandatory.
- With Titan or Fortified, scale and endurance must intensify the burden rather than create a heroic colossus.
- With Kaleidoscope, color behaves like damaged revelation and sensory punishment, never celebratory radiance.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: inward-folded weight and failed upward growth
- Surface: dirty wax, cracked gilding and bruised scar tissue
- Motion: reluctant light transferring wounds toward its source
- Cost mark: shame posture and fresh evidence of borrowed pain
- Visual pass: disgrace and weight before radiance
- Visual fail: clean halo hero

**Prop-free definition:** Unwanted grace preserves others by humiliating, exposing and physically degrading the being forced to channel it.

### Bulwark

**Mechanics anchor:** Tenacity with a high-tier first-control cleanse and protective response.

**Core fantasy:** Restraint survived and weaponized: a body that learned to resist control by internalizing the pressure once used to contain it.

**Emotional tone:** Controlled panic, stubborn refusal and the frightening stillness of someone waiting for restraint to snap.

**Silhouette verbs:** compress, lock, square.

**Silhouette language:** Shortened gaps, dense joints and lateral compression make the figure look difficult to move. Release should feel sudden because the resting state stores pressure.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Dense wrapped, quilted and braced construction
- Compression-scar bands and flattened tissue
- Block-jointed or low-slung nonhuman bodies
- Repair webbing that records repeated containment
- Bodies shaped by cages, institutional restraint or crushing environments without depicting the object itself

**Palette roles:** dominant: iron gray `#55595A`; support: prison green `#596754`; accent: old linen `#A39A83`; shadow: rust `#7C4A36`; cost: bruise plum `#5D4658`.

**Patterns:** compression bands, snapped rhythm, dense cross-bracing.

**Materials:** felted fiber, scar tissue, dull iron, cracked resin, packed hide.

**Magic behavior:** Control pressure accumulates visibly, then breaks in one sharp release. The response is bodily and mechanical—tension discharges, a locked rhythm skips—rather than a generic immunity glow.

**Supernatural cost:** Resistance leaves joints locked, breath shallow and sensation numbed. Each release reopens old restraint injuries or makes the body less capable of softness.

**Allowed variations:**

- institutional survivor
- brutal jailer
- compressed beast
- siege laborer
- panic-frozen aristocrat
- carapaced alien

**Forbidden cliches:**

- ordinary knight tank
- sports blocker
- generic chains everywhere
- clean heavy plate
- calm benevolent protector

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Bulwark owns compression, density and stored release.
- As secondary, use banding, joint behavior or a single snapped rhythm instead of adding bulk everywhere.
- With Aegis, Bulwark is internal resistance while Aegis is external enclosure.
- With Striker, released pressure becomes forward momentum but must retain visible restraint damage.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: compressed blocks with almost no loose negative space
- Surface: quilted density crossed by old pressure scars
- Motion: long stillness followed by one snapped release
- Cost mark: locked joints, flattened breath and reopened bands
- Visual pass: stored force in compressed mass
- Visual fail: ordinary castle plate

**Prop-free definition:** A compressed survivor stores the memory of restraint until attempted control triggers a violent bodily release.

### Cartel

**Mechanics anchor:** Team-wide bonuses derived from the costs represented on the field.

**Core fantasy:** Criminal loyalty enforced through valuation: belonging is purchased, status is visible and every relationship carries a price.

**Emotional tone:** Predatory intimacy, conspiratorial calm, class resentment and the security of being owned by dangerous people.

**Silhouette verbs:** cluster, angle, close.

**Silhouette language:** Figures imply closed ranks through inward angles, paired asymmetries and guarded social distance. Luxury appears repaired, inherited or stolen rather than pristine.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Predatory formal, labor-gang, prison-economy and decayed aristocratic construction
- Repaired luxury with visible ownership changes
- Paired markings or anatomical repetitions that imply a network
- Segmented nonhuman colonies governed by rank
- Bodies branded, altered or weighted according to value

**Palette roles:** dominant: oxblood `#6E2F32`; support: patinated jade `#466B5A`; accent: jaundiced ivory `#B5A67D`; shadow: cigarette gray `#6B6861`; cost: tarnished brass `#80633D`.

**Patterns:** account grids, shared cut marks, ranked serial repetition.

**Materials:** worn silk, felt, cracked lacquer, scarred hide, tarnished brass.

**Magic behavior:** Power moves across proximity and status. Advantage transfers like pressure through a closed network; value makes bodies visually heavier or more protected without summoning literal currency.

**Supernatural cost:** Identity becomes collateral. Members carry brands, shared pain, purchased repairs or bodily evidence that their survival belongs to the group.

**Allowed variations:**

- street syndicate
- decayed noble cabal
- prison economy
- family enterprise
- alien contract colony
- labor conspiracy
- ritual protection racket

**Forbidden cliches:**

- clean modern suit
- mafia costume
- coin rain
- gold bling as personality
- identical gang uniform
- cheerful criminal charm

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Cartel owns social geometry, rank and repaired luxury.
- As secondary, it appears through shared marks, material quality or ownership scars—not matching outfits.
- With Mogul, Cartel is networked value while Mogul is personal accumulation and spatial dominance.
- With Trader, Cartel is enforced belonging while Trader remains mobile, opportunistic and culturally mixed.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: inward angles and ranked proximity
- Surface: repaired luxury carrying multiple ownership scars
- Motion: pressure and advantage passing through a closed network
- Cost mark: identity branded as group collateral
- Visual pass: rank and ownership made structural
- Visual fail: coin-covered merchant

**Prop-free definition:** A closed criminal network converts rank, price and belonging into shared power while mortgaging every member's identity.

### Catalyst

**Mechanics anchor:** Post-battle item transformation through combining or evolution.

**Core fantasy:** Irreversible transformation: contact between unlike substances produces a new state that cannot return to what it was.

**Emotional tone:** Hungry curiosity, bodily trespass and exhilaration at becoming useful through permanent change.

**Silhouette verbs:** fuse, bloom, split.

**Silhouette language:** Asymmetry must show before and after states in one body. One side fuses, ruptures, flowers or vitrifies while the other retains the prior condition.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Fused seams joining incompatible materials
- Kiln-cracked ceramic or heat-warped bodies
- Botanical, fungal and biochemical chimera growth
- Industrial bodies shaped by reaction chambers without gadget clutter
- Textiles and skin permanently changed along a visible reaction front

**Palette roles:** dominant: heat copper `#9B603E`; support: furnace black `#242122`; accent: bone ceramic `#C1B79E`; shadow: opal oil `#6D746B`; cost: chemical green `#6D7C48`.

**Patterns:** reaction fronts, crystallization gradients, fused asymmetry.

**Materials:** heat-stained copper, cracked ceramic, slag, char, mutated tissue.

**Magic behavior:** Change begins at one contact point and travels across a material boundary. The result is a visible state change—fusing, vitrifying, charring, sprouting—not a cloud of alchemical accessories.

**Supernatural cost:** Every successful reaction leaves an irreversible mutation, fused joint, contaminated memory or incompatible material growing through the body.

**Allowed variations:**

- occult metallurgy
- biochemical mutation
- botanical grafting
- fungal conversion
- alien cocoon
- industrial reaction body
- ceramic transmuter

**Forbidden cliches:**

- generic steampunk inventor
- potion belt
- gear clutter
- rainbow alchemy
- clean laboratory coat
- floating ingredient cloud

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Catalyst owns visible state transition and fused asymmetry.
- As secondary, it changes one material or anatomical site chosen by the primary trait.
- A third trait may alter the result of the reaction but cannot add another transformation center.
- With Titan, transformation affects strata or communal mass; with Kaleidoscope, it affects perception before color saturation.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: one body split between prior and transformed states
- Surface: heat bloom crossing ceramic, slag and tissue
- Motion: a reaction front traveling from one contact point
- Cost mark: permanent fusion and incompatible growth
- Visual pass: two incompatible systems forced together
- Visual fail: sparkle alchemist

**Prop-free definition:** Contact triggers irreversible material and bodily change, making usefulness inseparable from contamination and lost original form.

### Chronomancer

**Mechanics anchor:** Allied acceleration and enemy slowing based on the number of Chronomancers.

**Core fantasy:** Stolen years and temporal decay: time is redistributed rather than controlled, leaving age, injury and memory out of order.

**Emotional tone:** Impatient exhaustion, nostalgia for events not yet lived and terror of arriving after one's own death.

**Silhouette verbs:** lag, repeat, taper.

**Silhouette language:** Contours echo at slightly wrong intervals; one region appears older, younger or delayed. The shape suggests a body failing to occupy one moment cleanly.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Mismatched-age anatomy across one body
- Layered eras of wear and repair
- Interrupted growth, regression or fossilization
- Faded duelist, laborer, court and nomadic constructions from incompatible periods
- Larval, fossil and senescent nonhuman stages coexisting

**Palette roles:** dominant: smoke blue `#566A78`; support: faded lavender `#817487`; accent: nicotine ivory `#B5A77D`; shadow: oxidized pewter `#6A706F`; cost: dead rose `#7C555E`.

**Patterns:** ghost repetition, interrupted stripes, uneven sun fading.

**Materials:** moth-eaten fiber, aged bone, corroded metal, faded dye, dry translucent skin.

**Magic behavior:** Motion arrives early or late. Afterimages misregister, dust briefly reverses and wounds wait before appearing; the behavior should never depend on visible clockwork.

**Supernatural cost:** Age distributes unevenly, memories lose sequence, injuries are deferred into worse moments and fatigue duplicates instead of resolving.

**Allowed variations:**

- young-old hybrid
- veteran trapped in one instant
- time parasite
- fossil larva
- decayed aristocrat
- accelerated beast
- future-haunted laborer

**Forbidden cliches:**

- floating clocks
- gear collage
- hourglass motif
- clean white-gold time knight
- blue portal rings
- perfect symmetrical afterimages

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Chronomancer owns repeated contour, age mismatch and delayed posture.
- As secondary, it changes the age or timing of one primary-trait channel.
- With Overload, time skips and drops rather than becoming continuous speed energy.
- With Exile, temporal displacement reinforces absence without defaulting to a traveler archetype.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: one contour repeated at unequal intervals
- Surface: faded layers aging at different rates
- Motion: early afterimage, delayed impact, brief reverse dust
- Cost mark: unequal age and memories out of sequence
- Visual pass: offset cadence visible in repeated intervals
- Visual fail: clock-face costume

**Prop-free definition:** Time is redistributed across one life, accelerating allies while leaving age, injury and memory permanently out of order.

### Executioner

**Mechanics anchor:** Critical lethality, kill stacking and high-tier bleed or protection-bypass behavior.

**Core fantasy:** Procedural death and official cruelty: killing treated as a final administrative action rather than a burst of rage.

**Emotional tone:** Cold certainty, practiced contempt and the calm of someone who believes an ending has already been authorized.

**Silhouette verbs:** descend, sever, finalize.

**Silhouette language:** Weight gathers above a decisive downward line. Neck, joint and termination zones are emphasized; the pose appears mid-verdict rather than stealthy or acrobatic.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Top-heavy or shearing anatomy built around downward force
- State, slaughterhouse, surgical and ritual-functionary constructions
- Dense collar and termination-line anatomy
- Insect, avian or machine-like predation organized around one final stroke
- Bodies scarred by repetitive authorized killing

**Palette roles:** dominant: iron black `#222225`; support: bone `#B9AE97`; accent: dried red `#6F3031`; shadow: execution green `#4E5B4C`; cost: ash `#77716C`.

**Patterns:** termination bars, tally gaps, single descending diagonals.

**Materials:** broad dull iron, worn wood grain, cured hide, bone, dried residue.

**Magic behavior:** Force compresses into one downward or severing event. Effects fall, split or stop abruptly; they do not orbit, flourish or advertise stealth.

**Supernatural cost:** The body stiffens into procedure, sensation dulls and victims leave persistent marks. Repetition makes reversal, mercy and lateral movement physically difficult.

**Allowed variations:**

- state functionary
- slaughter-beast
- surgical finalizer
- insect predator
- ritual judge
- industrial termination body
- silent civic killer

**Forbidden cliches:**

- hooded axe stereotype
- generic assassin
- skull-covered protection
- blood splash as sole identity
- nimble rogue silhouette
- theatrical rage

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Executioner owns downward mass and the single terminating line.
- As secondary, it contributes a finish rhythm or one anatomical shear point—not an occupational costume.
- With Vindicator, Vindicator strips resistance while Executioner ends the target.
- With Exile, lethal function survives after social identity and belonging have been removed.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: top weight collapsing into one downward line
- Surface: broad iron, bone and dried termination marks
- Motion: compression followed by an abrupt final stop
- Cost mark: stiffened procedure and victim traces that remain
- Visual pass: single decisive threshold and follow-through
- Visual fail: skull-and-axe emblem

**Prop-free definition:** Death becomes a calm authorized procedure expressed through descending force, severed continuity and a body losing mercy.

### Exile

**Core fantasy:** No-home survival and erased identity: power comes from living outside belonging, with exact gatherings briefly creating a substitute condition.

**Emotional tone:** Watchful isolation, severed pride, cultural grief and the dangerous self-sufficiency of someone no group can reclaim.

**Silhouette verbs:** drift, detach, misalign.

**Silhouette language:** One region appears separated from the rest by negative space, missing continuity or mismatched construction. The body never settles into a complete symmetrical belonging.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Mismatched repairs from incompatible places and eras
- Missing, blank or deliberately erased identity regions
- Weathered nomadic, court-banished, scavenged and institutional-survivor construction
- Separated or socially absent anatomy
- Nonhuman castoffs with incomplete colony, pack or hive morphology

**Palette roles:** dominant: road dust `#8B7D6A`; support: storm blue `#4D6170`; accent: faded heraldry `#76545C`; shadow: rust `#7D4C36`; cost: bone gray `#AAA79D`.

**Patterns:** interrupted borders, blank identity patches, stitched route fragments.

**Materials:** patched textile, weathered hide, sanded metal, scar tissue, dust-caked fiber.

**Magic behavior:** Position, attachment and continuity briefly detach. At exact counts, isolated rhythms align for one sharp upgrade, then remain visibly separate rather than becoming a matching faction.

**Supernatural cost:** Names, features, cultural memory or physical connections erode. The body cannot fully settle, heal into symmetry or be recognized by its former belonging.

**Allowed variations:**

- banished noble
- refugee predator
- alien castoff
- desert scavenger
- ghost without homeland
- packless beast
- institutional escapee

**Forbidden cliches:**

- generic hooded rogue
- lone samurai
- one tattered travel look
- map clutter
- romantic wandering hero
- uniformly dusty human nomad

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Exile owns absence, detachment and mismatched construction.
- As secondary, it removes or interrupts one channel belonging to the primary trait.
- Exact-count strength should intensify relational spacing, not produce identical clothing or anatomy.
- With Sanguine, exile changes the culture and psychology of hunger rather than merely weathering a vampire costume.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: a continuous body interrupted by deliberate absence
- Surface: incompatible repairs with erased identity patches
- Motion: drifting parts that align only at exact moments
- Cost mark: names, features and belonging worn away
- Visual pass: asymmetric absence that resists reunion
- Visual fail: hooded wanderer

**Prop-free definition:** Power comes from permanent unbelonging, with identity and bodily continuity eroded each time isolation becomes useful.

### Fortified

**Mechanics anchor:** Incoming damage reduction for members and, at higher tiers, allies.

**Core fantasy:** Siege architecture made flesh: survival through load-bearing structure, layered repair and the refusal to collapse under accumulated impact.

**Emotional tone:** Grounded fatalism, patient endurance and the grim dignity of becoming infrastructure for other people's survival.

**Silhouette verbs:** anchor, terrace, interlock.

**Silhouette language:** Mass settles downward through buttressed, layered forms. The body reads as load-bearing and repaired, not simply large or covered in conventional protection.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Buttressed torsos and load-bearing limbs
- Layered masonry, earth or densely woven tissue
- Embedded repair systems and visible structural patches
- Squat, columnar, quadruped and low-spread body plans
- Soft bodies made stable through tension, packing or interlock

- Form: asymmetric layers able to pivot into several routes
- Surface: patched histories from incompatible places
- Motion: possibilities fanning, swapping and hardening into one
- Cost mark: origin erased by too many successful exchanges
- Visual pass: reciprocal flow with visible loss each cycle
- Visual fail: market-arrow logo

**Prop-free definition:** Adaptation replaces bad possibilities through exchange, but every favorable bargain trades away another piece of origin and identity.

### Vindicator

**Mechanics anchor:** Attacks progressively strip physical and magical resistance.

**Core fantasy:** Ruined judgment and obsessive revenge: an accusation made bodily, peeling away every defense until only the condemned target remains.

**Emotional tone:** Cold grievance, tunnel vision, violated dignity and the terrible relief of reducing a complex world to one guilty thing.

**Silhouette verbs:** aim, accuse, pierce.

**Silhouette language:** A narrowed body commits to one diagonal line and one target. The posture should look accusatory and self-eroding, not righteous or heroically avenging.

**Garment/anatomy families:**

- Scarred court, military, civic, peasant and execution-survivor construction
- Diagonal anatomy and narrowed asymmetry
- Bodies etched by confession, testimony or repeated pursuit
- Immune-hunter and prey-specialist nonhuman forms
- Surfaces progressively stripped of protective layers

**Palette roles:** dominant: ash gray `#77716D`; support: slate `#4E5963`; accent: dried burgundy `#6B3D43`; shadow: old parchment `#AB9972`; cost: cold iron `#4B5052`.

**Patterns:** strike-throughs, verdict diagonals, erased seals.

**Materials:** scratched cloth, cold iron, cracked wax, scar tissue, soot.

**Magic behavior:** Every hit scrapes, scores or erases a visible layer of resistance. The effect carves and narrows toward the target instead of producing a righteous glow.

**Supernatural cost:** Obsession removes nuance, twists the body toward one quarry and corrodes the accuser with every judgment. Confession scars remain even when the target is gone.

**Allowed variations:**

- betrayed judge
- mob avenger
- alien immune hunter
- victim turned tyrant
- execution survivor
- obsessive investigator
- prey-specialist beast

**Forbidden cliches:**

- righteous paladin
- holy avenger
- generic dark knight
- glowing justice imagery
- red-eyed rage
- noble revenge hero

**Combination rules:**

- As primary, Vindicator owns the accusatory diagonal, narrowing and progressive stripping.
- As secondary, it erodes one defense or surface channel chosen by the primary trait.
- With Executioner, Vindicator exposes and strips while Executioner delivers final procedure.
- With Exile, revenge may grow from erased identity, but absence must remain distinct from accusation.

**Compact visual references:**

- Form: one narrowed diagonal committing the whole body
- Surface: layers scored, struck through and progressively erased
- Motion: each contact carving closer to an exposed core
- Cost mark: the accuser corroded into one permanent grievance
- Visual pass: all pressure narrows toward one condemned point
- Visual fail: angry revenge eye

**Prop-free definition:** Obsessive judgment strips away every defense while reducing the accuser's body, nuance and identity to one grievance.

## Combination quick rules

- First select an art-primary trait from gameplay identity and the strongest surviving concept idea.
- Assign secondary traits to material, pattern, palette, motion or cost channels that do not compete with the primary silhouette.
- A third trait receives one subtle corruption or rhythm only; never give all three traits equal visual volume.
- Combat role remains independently readable through stance, reach, speed and mass even when trait grammar is removed.
- Individual identity must still determine face, age, body type, species, culture and form of villainy.
- Test the design with color removed, effects removed and props hidden; at least one trait and the role must survive.
- If two traits both claim the same channel, the primary keeps it and the secondary moves to magic behavior or supernatural cost.
- No combination may erase the visible price of surviving this world.
