## Style Read

The user described the newer top-five unit art as having a Bloodborne-like and porcelain-looking style, which is meaningfully different from the darker unit direction already working for Vellum, Paisley, the token, and especially Creep.

The current Creep v5 is favored because it keeps:

- smooth alien skin/face instead of ripped-apart corpse anatomy,
- dissociated expression rather than theatrical hunter/drama,
- matte black scythe-ribbon silhouette,
- flat, dry, dark gothic finish,
- board-readable horror shape without shiny/sweaty material.

## Prompt Lesson

Future experiments can borrow from the Bloodborne/porcelain set only as a restrained secondary modifier:

```text
Take 10-15 percent Victorian gothic horror refinement and pale ceramic undertone,
but do not become porcelain statue, polished Bloodborne hunter, shiny leather,
wet skin, theatrical robe, clean cathedral fantasy, or ornate splash art.
Keep the Vellum matte material gate and Creep's smooth alien dissociation first.
```

For Creep-like units, do not average references. Use:

- Vellum first for matte material, dry detail richness, and non-shiny finish.
- Creep v5 for dissociated smooth-alien horror identity.
- New Bloodborne/porcelain references only for small doses of elegance, silhouette discipline, or tragic gothic atmosphere.
