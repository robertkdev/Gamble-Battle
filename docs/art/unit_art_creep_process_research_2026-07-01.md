## What Worked In The Creep Pass

The useful part of the Creep work was the process, not a definitive new global anchor:

1. Start with the original source sprite identity before style words.
2. Name the previous failure modes explicitly.
3. Use Vellum first for matte gothic material richness, dry detail, and de-shined finish.
4. Use later candidates only as evidence or negative comparisons, not as averaged style targets.
5. Add detail through surface weathering, dry pigment, scratches, chalk, dust, worn edges, and hand-painted breakup.
6. Reject both extremes: glossy/high-sheen rendering and simplified low-detail matte shapes.
7. Treat the result as a review candidate until a Vellum-first side-by-side review clears it.

For Creep, this meant restoring the smooth alien face and uninterrupted gray-blue skin first, then adding dry chalk pores, subtle mottling, hairline scratches, charcoal marks, and dull ink-black tendrils. The key lesson is identity first, then dry tactile detail. Do not solve a style failure by adding unrelated armor, costume scraps, or chaotic micro-detail.

## Applying It To Totem

Totem's existing failed candidate had a clean readable silhouette and acceptable palette, but still read too much like clean fantasy carved armor. The Totem research pass should preserve:

- jagged bark crown head
- cyan glowing eyes
- stern carved wooden face
- upright protective stance
- broad bark-plate torso
- turquoise spiral chest and limb markings
- bark-feather shoulder fringe
- long clawed wooden hands and feet

The style correction is to push the material away from polished wood armor and toward ancient dry bark, raw splintered wood, dusty dark grain, soot, chalky turquoise pigment, worn dull edges, dry brush, matte gouache, and heavier Vellum-like occlusion shadows.
