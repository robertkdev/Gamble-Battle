## Recommendation

`CONTINUE WORK`

The cut contains several strong, production-useful character premises, especially among the nonhuman units, but it is not yet a consistently professional twelve-unit calibration set. The largest problem is not missing lore: it is that the definitive images repeatedly fail to make the already-written psychology, villain relationship, supernatural price, and unit-specific threat legible without that lore.

## Material findings

1. **The specified survival psychology is not independently readable from any of the twelve face enlargements.**

   - **Observed evidence:** Before the psychology specification was revealed, E003, E007, E011, E015, E019, E023, E027, E031, E035, E039, E043, and E047 repeatedly resolved as a stern scowl, alien aperture, beauty-model seriousness, sly rogue smile, feral snarl, blank alien shorthand, sly goblin smirk, superior glamour half-smile, tough-guy sneer, calm performance, cool competence, or unoriented exposed organ. `PSYCHOLOGY_SPEC.json` records the later comparison to E_PSYCHOLOGY_SPEC: the internal wants, fears, active survival strategies, and contradictions were not recoverable unaided. Korath, Knoll, and Pilfer provide the clearest broad emotional direction, but even those faces do not expose the specified private motive or fear. The isolated B_MORWEN and the facial/action staging in B_RIOT_BRIAR demonstrate more specific target relationship and simultaneous emotional pressure, although the latter is a cinematic splash rather than a like-for-like neutral master.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §1 line 18; §7 lines 94–105; and especially §8 lines 111–131, including the rejection of generic scowls, smirks, snarls, blank seriousness, beauty-model expressions, and prose-only emotion.
   - **User consequence:** Players and downstream artists receive archetype attitude instead of twelve specific villains. The authored psychology becomes production documentation that must be explained beside the art rather than a reliable visual design.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Re-author each face and pose as one observable transaction with a target. Change eye focus, eyelid pressure, brow asymmetry, mouth/jaw state, head angle, weight, hand action, and prop contact together. Use each unit’s want/fear contradiction to select one unmistakable moment; do not attempt a face-only expression swap.

2. **Several humanoid masters read as neutral or familiar class archetypes before they read as villains with intentional adult threat.**

   - **Observed evidence:** Cashmere’s E009/E011 reads as a composed premium-fantasy witch; Knoll’s E025/E027 as a pleased clerk, merchant, or tax collector; Quillith’s E029/E031 as an inviting glamour caster or presenter; Luna’s E037/E039 as a graceful dancer or spell practitioner; and Sable’s E041/E043 as a competent dark-fantasy marksman. Korath’s open palm in E001 can plead or beckon, Veyra’s E005 can read as a sealed pod carrying a victim, Pilfer’s E013/E015 can read as an appealing rogue, and Kett’s E033/E035 can read as a conventional heroic brawler. By contrast, Nyxa E017, Creep E021, and Malachor E045 communicate hostile body logic more directly.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §1 lines 15–20; §4 lines 45–50 and 59; §9 lines 135–165; §11 lines 179–185.
   - **User consequence:** The roster can look attractively rendered yet emotionally interchangeable with heroes, NPCs, or neutral class representatives. That weakens the game’s villain-first promise and makes unit selection less immediately exciting.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Put a visible victim, target, boundary, or coercive consequence into the character’s action without adding a narrative scene. Make the dominant prop visibly act on someone or something: the stamp brands, the slate corrects or constrains, the thread divides or binds, the contract hand closes terms, and the rifle pose actually establishes a shot.

3. **The supernatural price is physically clear on only part of the roster and is too often indistinguishable from styling, equipment, or ordinary magic.**

   - **Observed evidence:** Korath E001, Veyra E005, Creep E021, and Malachor E045 visibly embody consequence in ruined anatomy, enclosure, surgical erasure, or exposed-organ architecture. Cashmere’s runes and black hand in E009 can be tattoos plus spell effect; Pilfer’s amber claws in E013 can be equipment; Nyxa’s split coloring in E017 has no visually established temporal cause; Knoll E025 shows no bodily or behavioral price; Quillith’s fissures in E029 and Luna’s lattice in E037 do not deform expression or movement; Kett’s E033 arm could be anatomy, armor, or temporary effect; Sable E041 reads as a skilled mundane rifle user.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §1 line 19; §12 lines 189–193; §14 line 216.
   - **User consequence:** The cruel-folklore world becomes a dark costume theme rather than a system of bargains and damage. Later animation and VFX teams would have to invent the cost instead of extending an authored master.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Make each price alter a load-bearing body or behavioral decision: restricted movement, asymmetric protection, compulsive contact, visible depletion, scarred functional anatomy, or a prop-body dependency. Remove decorative marks that do not change the action, or convert them into a visibly consequential mechanism.

4. **The roster has broad species variety but a conspicuous dark-tailored humanoid convergence, with Cashmere, Quillith, and Luna forming the tightest collision.**

   - **Observed evidence:** E049, E050_roster_style_convergence, E051_roster_style_convergence, and E052 show that Cashmere and Quillith share tall fit adult-female proportions, dark fitted garments, an extended open hand, and similarly sized rectangular occult objects; Luna repeats the tall fit woman plus open-hand caster grammar. At 96 pixels, E012, E032, and E040 become narrow upright dark figures whose distinguishing surface systems disappear. E053 shows several humanoids converging on black leather or cloth and document-adjacent props. Sable’s sealed E041/E043 read was explicitly feminine, while E_PSYCHOLOGY_SPEC assigns Sable a nonbinary-androgynous lane.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §1 line 20; §2 lines 24–29; §4 line 52; §5 lines 66–79; §14 line 215; §15 lines 223–235.
   - **User consequence:** The player sees a shared costume aisle and repeated social-caster pose rather than different people, cultures, and survival logics. Sable’s specified androgynous identity is not communicated by the image.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Preserve Cashmere, Quillith, and Luna as attractive, adult, lean/toned, feminine, and combat-capable while changing their governing shapes: different height and proportion emphasis, garment family, coverage/exposure location, color field, hand geometry, and prop-body relationship. Rebuild Sable’s face/body/garment silhouette so the androgynous lane reads without a label. Avoid solving convergence by making the women less attractive or less feminine.

5. **Five silhouette sheets do not perform the required exploration of three genuinely different premises.**

   - **Observed evidence:** Knoll E026 and Quillith E030 are pose variations of the same body, outfit, and prop. Kett E034 varies one fist mechanism around the same broad fighter. Luna E038 changes strand/effect packages around one dancer body. Sable E042 rotates one rifle around one long-coated figure. E049 confirms that these triptychs have materially less premise variation than Korath E002, Veyra E006, Pilfer E014, Nyxa E018, Creep E022, and Malachor E046.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §14 line 209, “Generate and compare three genuinely different rough silhouettes.”
   - **User consequence:** The selected master looks preselected; the sheet does not demonstrate that body plan, locomotion, protection, prop class, and dominant mass were challenged before polish. Downstream refinement is trapped inside a narrow initial premise.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Restart those triptychs from three different governing structures, not poses: change center of gravity, locomotion, external mass, prop-body integration, garment envelope, and attack line. Keep gameplay identity stable while making each option a genuinely different answer to it.

6. **Native-size reduction preserves several icons but removes the defining mechanism from roughly half the roster.**

   - **Observed evidence:** Korath E004, Kett E036, and Malachor E048 retain strong arch, fist, and pincer/body anchors. Veyra E008 becomes a seed or environmental pod; Cashmere E012 a generic woman with book; Knoll E028 a goblin with paper rather than a stamp action; Quillith E032 a woman with a case; Luna E040 a dancer with an ambiguous diagonal line; and Sable E044 a generic long-rifle shooter. Pilfer E016, Nyxa E020, and Creep E024 remain recognizable icons but lose species/action parsing. The full E051 board shows dark costumes merging with dark grounds across the humanoid subset.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §14 line 218; §16 line 244; the exact `96PX READ` criterion at §17 line 264; and the professional requirement for silhouette-and-96px readability.
   - **User consequence:** At actual play scale, multiple units retain class or prop but lose the thing that makes this specific character worth recognizing. Players must rely on labels, color memory, or later VFX.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Recompose from the 96-pixel target outward: enlarge one unit-specific body/prop relationship, separate it from the torso, increase large value grouping, simplify competing microdetail, and give dark figures a background-independent contour. Recheck the unchanged master rather than drawing a special icon substitute.

7. **Craft is often detailed, but focal and material hierarchy is inconsistent with the frozen professional references.**

   - **Observed evidence:** Knoll E025, Kett E033, and Malachor E045 distribute scales, straps, lines, ribs, cables, plates, and joints at similar frequency across most of the image; their action mechanics become harder to parse. E049/E051/E053 show recurring dark smoky grounds and dark garments compressing edges and material separation. B_VELLUM demonstrates that dense dry linework can remain organized by large parchment arcs, shoulder/coat mass, and separated material blocks; B_MORWEN uses a clear cape-versus-sword counterbalance within a similarly dark dry material lane. B_RIOT_CAMILLE and B_RIOT_BELVETH are not composition matches, but their large directional masses and focal value structures remain unambiguous beneath high detail.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §13 lines 197–203 and the stated professional bar for polished, technically coherent, downstream-usable concept art.
   - **User consequence:** Detail initially signals expense, then becomes visual noise. Modelers, animators, and UI artists have less certainty about which surfaces, mechanisms, and edges must survive.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Reblock each master into three to five large value/material regions before restoring detail. Reserve the sharpest edge contrast and highest detail density for the face, dominant prop/anatomy, and visible cost; simplify secondary garment and underbody fields.

8. **The comparison evidence is answer-bearing and does not include a clean per-unit three-nearest-neighbor or trait-channel proof.**

   - **Observed evidence:** E050 places intended wants, fears, strategies, emotions, contradictions, face directions, and forbidden reads beside every face. E052 prints lane, prop/anatomy, visible cost, emotion, and contradiction beside each master. E053 labels the reference function and gives authored comparison instructions. E049 and E051 also print names, role/trait labels, and selection labels. E_PSYCHOLOGY_SPEC names three neighbors per unit, but no inspected comparison artifact isolates those three neighbors for each unit; no inspected board demonstrates one art-primary trait plus separately readable secondary channels.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** The lore-free inspection rule at §8 lines 111–119; §14 lines 212 and 217; §16 lines 239–250.
   - **User consequence:** A reviewer can be primed to see the intended answer, while the most important convergence and trait questions remain unsupported by clean direct evidence. Missing proof risks treating labels as successful visual communication.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Produce image-only face, native-size, role, trait, and per-unit nearest-neighbor boards. Keep authored interpretation in a separately opened sheet after the blind read. For every unit, show the selected master beside exactly its three named neighbors at master and 96-pixel scale, and identify primary versus secondary trait channels only after the image-only comparison.

9. **The machine-readable review contract is not reliably executable from its frozen location and does not fully validate the blank contract it accompanies.**

   - **Observed evidence:** E054 is a blank three-seat template under `docs/art/phase2_calibration/audit_contract/unit_template.json`. E055 computes `ROOT = Path(__file__).resolve().parents[2]`; from E055’s frozen location that is `docs/art`, after which its constants append `docs/art` again. The resulting criteria and default-template paths contain `docs/art/docs/art`. E055’s `DEFAULT_TEMPLATE` also names a different file from E054. Its `validate_template` path checks only a subset of E054’s nested keys, and its optional file checks establish PNG container/hash/dimensions rather than whether declared crop or containment transforms actually produced the image. No completed per-unit three-seat records are present in the reviewed cut.
   - **Violated criterion or professional expectation:** §17 lines 279–291, including the required starting template, criteria provenance, derivative/source provenance, adversarial validation, distinct review evidence, and completed-record validation.
   - **User consequence:** The audit can appear rigorous while the shipped validator targets the wrong paths or accepts a structurally incomplete blank template. Review durability and downstream approval provenance are not dependable.
   - **Practical repair direction:** Make the validator resolve the repository root from its actual installed location, point its default template to the reviewed canonical template, validate every declared nested template field and enum, and add in-place self-tests for the exact frozen paths. Then create completed per-unit records and run file-backed validation; keep transformation-content limits explicit rather than implying that PNG/hash checks prove visual derivation.

## Strongest contrary point

A reasonable reviewer could argue that this is already a valuable calibration cut because Korath, Veyra, Pilfer, Nyxa, Creep, Kett, and Malachor have memorable dominant silhouettes; Korath, Veyra, Creep, and Malachor embody supernatural damage; Kett and Sable communicate combat role immediately; the roster has substantial species breadth; and much of the rendering uses dry, worn, western-gothic materials. That argument is strongest when the work is treated as exploratory concept art rather than approval-ready masters. It does not resolve the decisive contrary evidence: the locked standard explicitly requires the definitive master, same-master face, and 96-pixel derivative to communicate specific psychology, villain relationship, supernatural cost, and roster identity now, without explanatory prose or future production carrying the missing read.
