# Recommendation

`CONTINUE WORK`

The cut contains several strong large-form hooks, especially among the nonhuman units, but it is not yet reliable as professional concept-lock material. The dominant user-facing problem is that the twelve complete psychology records explain specificity that the unaided images frequently do not communicate. Several humanoids also converge on the same dark-costume, restrained-expression, open-hand or document-prop solution, and the small-scale derivatives preserve class props more often than individual identity.

# Material findings

## 1. The specified survival psychologies do not read unaided from the definitive faces

**Evidence:** Across E003, E007, E011, E015, E019, E023, E027, E031, E035, E039, E043, and E047, Seat C could not recover the complete current feeling, want, fear, active survival strategy, contradiction, and villainous distortion later revealed in E_PSYCHOLOGY_SPEC. The visible reads instead included generic anger, fatigue, grief, or sternness for Korath; teeth-only hunger or pain for Veyra; composed beauty for Cashmere; impish mischief for Pilfer; a generic predatory snarl for Nyxa; broad curiosity, caution, sadness, or predation for Creep; calm concentration or gentle amusement for Knoll; confident friendliness for Quillith; confidence or taunting for Kett; coy calm for Luna; generic concentration for Sable; and exposed-organ vulnerability or machinery for Malachor. E050 shows the same convergence side by side.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** Locked criteria §7 (lines 92-108), §8 `PSYCHOLOGY READS FROM FACE` and `FACE AND POSE AGREE` (lines 109-132), §9 (lines 133-166), and the professional requirement that a concept be psychologically authored without explanatory apology.

**User consequence:** A player receives a generic archetype or mood while the production team receives a detailed psychology paragraph. That gap is expensive: later animation, portrait, narrative, and marketing work would be asked to invent the missing read rather than inherit it from the master.

**Practical repair direction:** Rebuild each affected master from the already-written face and pose directions as one coupled action. Make eye target, eyelid tension, mouth and jaw loading, head angle, asymmetry, weight line, prop contact, and the viewer relationship visibly encode the same want and fear. Re-derive the face crop and 96-pixel image only after the master changes; explanatory labels must remain hidden during the next unaided read.

## 2. Too many units read as sympathetic, occupational, performative, or heroic before they read as villains

**Evidence:** Korath's open palm and bowed head read as pleading (E001-E003); Veyra's bound central form can read as a captive inside a passive shell (E005-E008); Cashmere reads as a controlled occult scholar (E009-E012); Pilfer's smile softens the lunge into crafty amusement (E013-E016); Creep's head cant and open crouch can read as wary or vulnerable (E021-E024); Knoll reads as a clerk calmly stamping paper (E025-E028); Quillith reads as a friendly demonstrator or recruiter (E029-E032); Luna reads as a dancer or controlled presenter (E037-E040); Sable reads as a disciplined mercenary or protagonist (E041-E044). Kett clearly reads as a brawler, but the grin does not isolate villainous grievance (E033-E035).

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §1 governing villain and survival-psychology read (lines 11-21), §2 dangerous lane construction (lines 22-32), §8, §11 `VILLAIN` and `HORROR/THREAT` (lines 177-186), and §14 role-supporting combat silhouette (lines 205-220).

**User consequence:** The roster can feel like a collection of darkly dressed jobs rather than a cast of dangerous antagonists worth recruiting. The user must be told that a stamp, slate, filament, book, or open palm is coercive because the action itself does not make the coercion visible.

**Practical repair direction:** Show the exact victim, target, distance-control decision, or bodily consequence implied by the pose without adding floating explanation. Korath's hand must collect rather than beg; Veyra must visibly control its contents; Pilfer's reach must perform theft rather than playful claw display; Knoll's stamp must visibly impose a brand or obligation; Quillith's correction must constrain; Luna's bridge must split or control two parties; Sable's gaze and rifle must agree on a proof-target.

## 3. The humanoid roster normalizes one dark costume aisle and two repeated presentation patterns

**Evidence:** E049, E050, E052, and E053 show Cashmere, Quillith, Luna, and Sable sharing tall human proportions, fitted black or plum clothing, narrow waists, long legs, restrained faces, and studio poses. Cashmere and Quillith both use an attractive standing woman, a rectangular occult document object, fitted dark trousers, and an extended hand. Knoll adds another pale paper or document action. E053 shows the document family colliding with the Vellum anchor, while dark leather, straps, boots, long panels, and smoky backgrounds recur more broadly. At E051, Cashmere and Quillith remain especially close as standing dark women with one arm and one rectangular object.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §1 unique roster member (lines 11-21), §4 varied female body and styling range (lines 41-63), §5 character-specific appeal (lines 64-80), §14 unique body plan, garment family, prop, emotion, and combat silhouette (lines 205-220), and §15 anti-convergence vetoes (lines 221-236).

**User consequence:** The player must rely on names and role labels to distinguish several units, while the roster loses the collectible pleasure of immediately different people, cultures, bodies, and combat logics. The attractive women also become variations of controlled dark-caster beauty rather than separate dangerous personalities.

**Practical repair direction:** Give Cashmere, Quillith, and Luna different body rhythm, garment construction, spatial footprint, action line, and dominant mechanism rather than merely changing markings or hand position. Keep only one document-centered dominant idea in this cluster; let another unit's identity move into anatomy, culturally specific clothing, or a combat action that remains distinct at 96 pixels. Separate Sable's clothing family from the same long dark tailored lane even though the rifle already distinguishes role.

## 4. Supernatural cost often reads as decoration, species anatomy, or unexplained equipment

**Evidence:** Cashmere's script and black fluid could be tattoo, power, corruption, or ornament (E009-E012); Pilfer's translucent fingertips are materially unexplained (E013-E016); Creep's tendrils could be anatomy, blades, shadows, or an effect (E021-E024); Knoll's stamp has no visible harmful consequence (E025-E028); Quillith's copper network and Luna's pale lattice appear decorative because neither body visibly reacts (E029-E032, E037-E040); Kett's enlarged arm and marks do not resolve as mutation, gauntlet, machinery, or consequence (E033-E036); Sable's scars and rifle wraps are not behaviorally bound to the stated grievance (E041-E044). Even stronger cost-bearing designs retain ambiguity: Korath's bodily ruin is visible but its failed-miracle debt is not (E001-E004), Veyra's enclosure can be ordinary species construction (E005-E008), and Nyxa's temporal split can be lighting or patterning (E017-E020). Malachor is the clearest body-ruin case (E045-E048), though its maintenance panic is not visible.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §1 supernatural consequence, §12 `SUPERNATURAL COST` (lines 187-194), and §14's requirement to show role and price without accessory clutter.

**User consequence:** The cruel-folklore world becomes a surface style rather than a system of bargains and damage. Tattoos, cracks, cords, paper, and black fluid risk becoming interchangeable gothic decoration.

**Practical repair direction:** Choose one causal cost per unit and make it physically alter weight bearing, anatomy, contact, breath, pain management, material wear, or the combat action. Remove secondary marks that do not clarify that cause. The cost must remain inferable with labels hidden and must not depend on a future VFX layer.

## 5. Native-size images preserve generic class props more often than personal identity

**Evidence:** E004-E048 and E051 show that Korath's arch, Veyra's pod, Nyxa's bow and ears, Creep's radial tendrils, Kett's fist, Malachor's pincer, and Sable's rifle survive as large shapes. However, faces, psychology, material distinctions, skin marks, supernatural-cost cues, and garment construction collapse throughout. Cashmere becomes a generic book caster (E012), Knoll a paper-and-stamp clerk (E028), Quillith a board-carrying caster (E032), Luna a dancer or martial caster (E040), and Sable a generic rifle shooter (E044). E051 also keeps answer-bearing names, roles, and trait labels beside every icon and does not show battlefield clutter, selection states, overlap, health bars, or motion.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §14 validate the unchanged master at 96 pixels (lines 205-220), §16 native-size evidence (lines 237-251), and the exact `96PX READ` gate in §17 (lines 252-293).

**User consequence:** In actual roster scanning, the most important repeated units are likely to be identified by weapon class or adjacent text, not by person, psychology, or supernatural logic. That makes upgrades, targeting, and collection memory more brittle.

**Practical repair direction:** Recompose the masters so each unit has a single high-area, high-contrast personal hook that is not merely the generic role prop. Test icon-only derivatives on representative battlefield values without labels, then adjust pose, negative space, and local value grouping at master level.

## 6. Several silhouette sheets vary gesture or prop placement rather than exploring genuinely different constructions

**Evidence:** Cashmere's candidates retain the same slim adult, tailoring, bun, and book family (E010); Knoll's candidates retain the same body, costume, stamp, paper, and compact two-hand action (E026); Quillith's retain the same body, outfit, board, and open-hand vocabulary (E030); Kett mainly swaps fist, drill, and barrel endpoints on the same large body (E034); Luna's first two candidates retain the same dancer body and thin thread identity, while the third changes protection logic abruptly (E038); Sable's candidates remain familiar standing, vertical, and low rifle poses around the same shooter (E042).

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §14 requires three genuinely different rough silhouettes and one dominant prop or anatomy (lines 205-220); §16.1 requires those three possibilities in the packet.

**User consequence:** The selection step gives an illusion of exploration while preserving the first normalized assumptions. Later feedback can only tune a hand, prop, or stance instead of choosing among materially different identity solutions.

**Practical repair direction:** For the affected units, explore three alternatives that change at least body rhythm, negative-space strategy, action axis, and how the supernatural mechanism occupies the silhouette while preserving the same gameplay facts. Do not change the combat mechanism so radically that the alternatives cease to represent one unit.

## 7. Material hierarchy and focal control fall below the frozen professional comparisons

**Evidence:** Korath's arch, hair, torso, arm, and background compress into similar values (E001-E004); Cashmere, Knoll, Quillith, Luna, and Sable lose lower-body separation in dark clothing (E009-E012, E025-E032, E037-E044); Nyxa's bow, cords, limbs, and face overlap densely (E017-E020); Creep's tendrils merge into a decorative ring and background (E021-E024); Kett and Malachor distribute high-frequency marks, ribs, plates, cords, and textures across most of the body (E033-E036, E045-E048). In contrast, B_RIOT_BRIAR, B_RIOT_BELVETH, and B_RIOT_CAMILLE use controlled broad value groups and a clear face-to-action path; B_VELLUM separates worn leather, cloth, paper, wax, skin, metal, and feather with restrained highlights; B_MORWEN separates scarred skin, worn leather, bone, cloth, and blade while keeping the face dominant.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §13 Vellum material/detail/grounded-realism veto and dry premium finish (lines 195-204), plus the shared brief's professional standard for coherence, specificity, and production usability.

**User consequence:** The designs look busier but less readable than the comparison bar. Fine detail becomes expensive to reproduce while failing to improve recognition or emotional hierarchy.

**Practical repair direction:** Repaint around two or three broad material/value groups per unit, reserve the highest local contrast for face or deliberate nonhuman attention mechanism and combat action, and delete repeated microtexture that does not explain anatomy, cost, or role. Compare like-for-like character crops rather than cinematic atmosphere alone.

## 8. The packet's roster boards explain the intended answer beside the evidence

**Evidence:** E049 prints names, roles, traits, and selected-silhouette letters. E050 places private want, fear, survival strategy, current emotion, contradiction, face direction, and forbidden read beside every face. E051 places names, roles, traits, and primary traits beside every 96-pixel image. E052 prints lane, prop or anatomy, visible cost, emotion, and contradiction. E053 labels the reference function and prints lane, emotion, and dominant-idea claims. Crop scales and image fitting also vary across E049-E053.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §8 requires a without-lore face read; §14 requires nearest-neighbor and roster comparison; §16.8-10 requires comparison evidence; the Board skill requires direct evidence that does not disclose answer-bearing annotations before review.

**User consequence:** A reviewer can mistake successful reading of captions for successful reading of art. The packet is useful as an explanation board but brittle as independent evidence, especially for the exact psychology and 96-pixel gates.

**Practical repair direction:** Produce clean image-only face, master, nearest-neighbor, and icon boards with equalized use scale and no names, roles, traits, lane labels, selected letters, psychology, or intended interpretations. Seal observations first, then reveal an identically ordered answer key.

## 9. The machine-readable audit contract can create assurance without proving semantic review quality

**Evidence:** In E055, `Path(__file__).resolve().parents[2]` resolves from the frozen file to `docs/art`, producing nonexistent `docs/art/docs/art/...` criteria and template paths; completed-record validation therefore cannot complete its criteria-byte comparison at this location. E055 accepts round-one and cross-examination JSON as any nonempty object or list, reduces independence to unique IDs and distinct path/hash strings, validates prose mainly by minimum length and enums, checks only PNG signature and IHDR, and does not compare derivative pixels with the master. E054 does not model evaluator family, instance, session, clean context, transcript, per-artifact inspection receipts, criterion-to-evidence bindings, or benchmark IDs and hashes.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §17 machine-readable review enforcement (lines 252-293), especially canonical criteria bytes, independent attribution, same-master derivation, benchmark-calibrated evidence, cross-examination clearance, and validated completed records.

**User consequence:** A formally green record could still contain three nominal identities, arbitrary nonempty review JSON, invented benchmark prose, unrelated valid PNGs, or unverified crops. More immediately, the frozen validator's root bug prevents its advertised path from establishing the criteria binding at all.

**Practical repair direction:** Resolve repository paths from an explicit repository-root argument or discovered marker; validate a formal schema for each external seat and cross-examination record; bind seat/session/context and evidence receipts; decode complete images; compare deterministic derivatives with the selected master; require benchmark and criterion references to resolve; exercise one completed record plus adversarial semantic substitutions at the exact frozen artifact path.

## 10. Two identity gates conflict with the unaided read

**Evidence:** Knoll's deep eyelid, forehead, cheek, and mouth creasing produced an older or fatigued face, while E_PSYCHOLOGY_SPEC assigns equivalent age 31 (E025-E028). Sable's master was unaidedly described as a mature woman, while E_PSYCHOLOGY_SPEC assigns a nonbinary androgynous visual lane (E041-E044). Nyxa's age displacement remains recognizably tied to a dangerous batlike predator rather than an ordinary frail elder (E017-E020), so the concern is not a blanket objection to visible age in a deliberate horror lane.

**Violated criterion/professional expectation:** §2 allowed lane fidelity (lines 22-32), §3 `PEAK-AGE FIGHTER` (lines 33-40), and §14's unique face/body/species logic.

**User consequence:** Knoll risks presenting the forbidden elderly merchant or grandfather read, while Sable's intended identity is not reliably communicated by the selected master. Both mismatches would propagate into portraits and animation if concept lock treats the text as proof.

**Practical repair direction:** For Knoll, retain adult goblin specificity while removing ordinary old-age creasing and sharpening active target focus. For Sable, adjust face, body language, tailoring, and silhouette so the intended androgynous adult lane survives without relying on a label, while preserving the rifle role and avoiding a generic masculine substitution.

# Strongest contrary point

The strongest concern is not that the cut lacks ideas; it is that the documentation and boards make the ideas feel more visible than they are. When names, roles, trait labels, psychology paragraphs, intended emotions, and comparison instructions are removed, the user often sees a plea, a beauty portrait, a mischievous rat, an administrator, a friendly instructor, a dancer, a generic bruiser, or a conventional sniper. A complete psychology spec, many comparison boards, hashes, and a formal validator can therefore increase confidence while masking the central adoption failure: the definitive pictures do not yet carry enough of the promised identity on their own.
