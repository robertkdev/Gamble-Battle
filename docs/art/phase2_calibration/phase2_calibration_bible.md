# Phase 2 — Real-Unit Calibration Concepts

## Purpose and acceptance envelope

This packet stress-tests the Phase 1 trait system on twelve real roster units. It is concept art only: one rough silhouette triptych and one definitive full-body master concept per unit. The only derivative is a deterministic 96 px board-read check cropped from that same master. No shop cards, headshots, sprite sheets, animation sheets, or VFX assets are in scope.

The twelve units jointly cover all 22 traits, every combat role, two-trait and three-trait combinations, human/monstrous/alien body plans, multiple ages/genders/body types, and attractive/grotesque/feral/nonhuman lanes. Existing gameplay identity and the strongest recognizable hook survive; the calibration intentionally does not preserve weak costumes.

Global gates:

- Every character reads as a villain or dangerous antagonist, but villain emotion varies: shame, administrative contempt, acquisitive calculation, predatory opportunism, feral vacancy, cold instruction, labor rage, manipulative intimacy, or bodily panic.
- Dry tactical-gothic finish: dusty matte cloth, smoked bone, oxidized metal, bruised flesh, soot, wax, old paper, and mineral surfaces. No porcelain skin, glossy gacha polish, clean-fantasy glamour, or decorative particle halos.
- One dominant prop or anatomical idea per unit. Secondary traits occupy separate channels and may not add a second competing prop.
- Magic must originate from a body, garment, structure, or chosen prop. Floating effects cannot carry trait identity.
- Board read is judged from the unchanged master at 96 px. Tiny details may reward close view but cannot rescue silhouette, role, or identity.
- Neighbor collision check is against the other eleven calibration units plus the retained Vellum/Paisley/Creep/Morwen world anchors. No shared black-coat wardrobe, identical hourglass bodies, repeated horns, repeated wings, repeated orbiting objects, or one universal angry face.

## Unit records

### Korath — Titan / Blessed — Tank

- Gameplay identity: diverts nearby allies' damage into himself, stores pressure, then dashes, stuns, heals an ally, and releases the punishment.
- Art-primary trait: **Blessed**, carried by bowed posture, ruined devotional anatomy, and compulsory penance.
- Secondary channel: **Titan** lives only in immense load-bearing mass, feet, shoulders, and ground pressure; it adds no heroic armor.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** broken processional arch carried across the shoulders; **B** kneeling reliquary hunch with forearms planted; **C** bell-shaped stone penitent with split mantle. **Selected: A**, because the lowered head under a crushing horizontal burden reads disgrace before angel and tank before saint.
- Survival psychology: Korath protects others because every wound he steals proves that the failed miracle still needs him; he cannot distinguish service from self-punishment.
- Villain read: coercive martyr who makes allies indebted to his suffering.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one fused penitential yoke/reliquary beam, grown through both shoulders.
- Supernatural cost: redirected wounds calcify as pale stone blooms, goldless stigmata fissures, and collapsed sensation; his face remains visible, exhausted, and ashamed.
- Palette/material: ash limestone, bruised umber flesh, old linen, smoked bone, minute dead-ochre residue. No bright gold, white wings, halo disc, clean plate, or radiant backlight.
- 96 px survival: bowed head trapped below the long shoulder beam, giant planted feet, one bright cracked chest wound.
- Blessed gate: fallen/disgraced first; shame visible in face and posture; miracle cost physical; no holy hero, paladin, black-wing demon, or pretty sad angel.

### Veyra — Aegis / Bulwark — Tank

- Gameplay identity: converts Aegis stacks into short flat damage reduction, then permanent combat max-health gain.
- Art-primary trait: **Aegis**, expressed as nested living enclosures folding around a vulnerable center.
- Secondary channel: **Bulwark** appears in compressed square buttresses and locking joints, never a carried shield.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** tall folding gate with nested forearms; **B** low six-limbed mobile bastion; **C** cocoon torso on two pillar legs. **Selected: C**, giving a memorable broad tank body without resembling Korath's horizontal burden.
- Survival psychology: Veyra survives by sealing every new injury behind another layer until protection becomes imprisonment.
- Villain read: possessive protector that treats allies as contents, not people.
- Primary prop/anatomy: no prop; the rib-cocoon shell is the dominant anatomical idea.
- Supernatural cost: each hardening permanently fuses one joint and deadens a sensory slit.
- Palette/material: chalky ceramic shell without shine, blue-gray tendon, oxidized copper seams, dim plum core.
- 96 px survival: two pillar legs, huge closed oval torso, tiny off-center head aperture.

### Cashmere — Arcanist / Mogul — Mage

- Gameplay identity: fires an arcane blast; a kill can pay out gold.
- Art-primary trait: **Mogul**, expressed through occupied vertical mass, accumulated layers, and ownership posture.
- Secondary channel: **Arcanist** lives in measured diagram cuts, suspended cuffs, and a single controlled casting line across the ledger.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** broad seated-to-standing column with high ledger mantle; **B** soft triangular coatless drape around one huge book; **C** top-heavy counting-frame torso with narrow feet. **Selected: B**, preserving elegant authority while escaping the black-suit/long-coat family.
- Survival psychology: Cashmere converts uncertainty into accounts because anything priced can be owned, including people and spells.
- Villain read: warm, patient acquisition; she smiles only when someone becomes an asset.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one cracked arcane ledger clasped under the left arm.
- Supernatural cost: every paid kill erases a personal memory and prints it as black tax-script through her fingers and throat.
- Palette/material: tobacco velvet, parchment linen, bruised mauve, verdigris brass, ink-blackened skin; no generic black trench coat, floating coins, spectacles-as-entire-identity, or orb.
- 96 px survival: broad asymmetric drape, rectangular ledger block, one ink-dark casting arm.

### Pilfer — Catalyst / Cartel — Assassin

- Gameplay identity: dashes through the farthest enemy, becomes briefly untargetable, cuts/disrupts, and steals Catalyst charge.
- Art-primary trait: **Catalyst**, visible as one body split into incompatible reaction states.
- Secondary channel: **Cartel** is encoded in repeated cut marks, closed-angle body language, and a single stolen exchange pouch.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** forward wedge with oversized reagent sleeve; **B** split-tailed ratfolk runner with one long siphon hand; **C** compact crouch beneath a triangular hood-sack. **Selected: B**, making the dash line and stolen charge readable without ninja weapons.
- Survival psychology: Pilfer stays alive by becoming whatever the room rewards before anyone can decide what they are.
- Villain read: ingratiating opportunist who steals identity as readily as currency.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one elongated siphon hand with fold-out ampoule fingers; no separate dagger set.
- Supernatural cost: each theft chemically mismatches another patch of skin, fur, voice, and limb temperature.
- Palette/material: nicotine cloth, copper salts, sour green residue, rust red cartel cuts, diseased cream fur.
- 96 px survival: low sprinting wedge, split tail, one disproportionately long siphon arm.

### Nyxa — Sanguine / Chronomancer — Marksman

- Gameplay identity: turns four attacks into chaotic multi-arrow volleys and adds another arrow on each cast.
- Art-primary trait: **Chronomancer**, expressed by one body at incompatible ages along a controlled long-range axis.
- Secondary channel: **Sanguine** lives in predatory apertures, clotted string, and blood exchanged for each additional arrow.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** old batlike archer with crescent spine-bow; **B** four-armed narrow tower whose lower arms age backward; **C** wingless membrane cloak stretched into a bow. **Selected: A**, preserving Nyxa's feral demon ancestry while rejecting generic horned gothic anatomy.
- Survival psychology: Nyxa hoards possible futures as ammunition because choosing one life would mean admitting the others are dead.
- Villain read: ecstatic predator who experiences victims as rehearsal timelines.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one asymmetrical time-bow grown from a calcified wing finger and spine.
- Supernatural cost: every extra arrow ages one side of her face and drains blood backward into older scars.
- Palette/material: dried carmine membrane, soot-black fur, nicotine bone, cold lilac age bruising; no skull trinkets, horns, tail clutter, or glossy demon plate.
- 96 px survival: long diagonal bow, folded batlike hunch, one pale ancient half-face.

### Creep — Exile / Executioner — Assassin

- Gameplay identity: dives the lowest-health enemy, spins rapid physical area damage, becomes unstoppable/damage-reduced, and can extend or chain through Exile.
- Art-primary trait: **Exile**, expressed through an incomplete outline, displaced center, and erased identity surfaces.
- Secondary channel: **Executioner** occupies the matte scythe-ribbon anatomy and decisive collapsing arc.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** low crouch inside a broken ribbon ring; **B** vertical needle body with ribbons hanging as a guillotine; **C** four-point crawl with one sweeping crescent. **Selected: A**, preserving the approved hollow-eyed alien identity and strongest board read.
- Survival psychology: Creep survives by removing every trace that could let a group claim or reject it, then hunts the most vulnerable witness.
- Villain read: vacant, procedural predation rather than anger.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one broken ring of matte black scythe-ribbons grown from the spine.
- Supernatural cost: each chain execution erases a facial contour and cuts another identity mark from smooth gray-blue skin.
- Palette/material: matte charcoal ribbons, dry gray-blue skin, black eye voids, faint bone-white scars; no armor, clothing clutter, shine, horns, teeth, or new props.
- 96 px survival: hollow eyes, low triangular crouch, broken black blade ring.

### Knoll — Trader / Harmony — Support

- Gameplay identity: marks one enemy with stun, armor/resistance shred, and a ledger strike.
- Art-primary trait: **Trader**, expressed through a narrow exchange gap and measured valuation gesture.
- Secondary channel: **Harmony** lives in paired counterweights and repeated intervals on the body, not musical decoration.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** short rectangular magistrate with one tall marking iron; **B** broad goblin scale-body with paired hanging sleeves; **C** hunched receipt-clerk whose back forms an abacus arc. **Selected: A**, preserving the merchant/accounting hook while removing comic prop clutter.
- Survival psychology: Knoll survives by making every relationship transactional before affection can become leverage against him.
- Villain read: courteous bureaucrat who destroys lives with a precise valuation.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one long receipt-branding iron, also used as a support cane.
- Supernatural cost: every marked enemy adds a paired tally scar across his eyelids and shortens his depth perception.
- Palette/material: faded brick wool, dirty receipt cream, tarnished nickel, muted teal counter-stitch, liver-spotted green skin.
- 96 px survival: short square body, vertical iron, two balanced sleeve weights, one squinting monocular face.

### Quillith — Scholar / Overload / Mentor — Support

- Gameplay identity: names a Pupil, protects and amplifies them, then forces a reduced recast with team mana spillover.
- Art-primary trait: **Mentor**, carried by an authority-forward looming posture and one directed correction line.
- Secondary channels: **Scholar** is the layered notation woven into a rigid support harness; **Overload** is confined to frayed nerves, crowded script, and dangerous spill at the head and hands.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** tall arthritic teacher braced by a mobile lectern frame; **B** seated triangular examiner in a wheeled correction chair; **C** narrow human figure beneath a vast crooked grading arm. **Selected: A**, giving disability, age, and instructional dominance without floating books or wings.
- Survival psychology: Quillith keeps pupils dependent because being surpassed would make every sacrifice of her own body meaningless.
- Villain read: intimate, punitive control disguised as instruction.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one fused lectern-brace that supports her spine and projects the correction line.
- Supernatural cost: every forced recast burns another lesson into exposed nerves and makes her own speech leak as overlapping annotations.
- Palette/material: smoke-brown wool, chalk dust, blackened oak, faded ultramarine notation, raw red nerve thread.
- 96 px survival: tall tilted frame, tiny severe head, one long corrective arm, rigid triangular base.

### Kett — Striker / Cartel — Brawler

- Gameplay identity: lands a three-punch sequence that stacks on-hit pressure, ramps, and leaves a frontline debuff.
- Art-primary trait: **Striker**, expressed as a forward-loaded single vector and three visible impact stages in one arm.
- Secondary channel: **Cartel** lives in stamped ownership scars and a closed triangular stance, not gangster clothing.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** broad dockworker with one triple-jointed stamp fist; **B** low charging shoulder with forearm piled like a ram; **C** upright debt enforcer with three nested knuckle plates. **Selected: A**, preserving Kett's blunt worker identity while deleting the competing shield.
- Survival psychology: Kett survives humiliation by making every conflict simple enough to solve in three blows.
- Villain read: controlled labor rage that becomes relief only at impact.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one oversized wage-stamp prosthetic fist with three nested striking plates.
- Supernatural cost: each punch embosses another debt coin into forearm bone and reduces finger movement.
- Palette/material: tarred canvas, oxidized iron, tobacco leather, dull brass stamps, bruised working skin; no long black coat, paygate shield, wrestler mask, or second weapon.
- 96 px survival: huge forward fist, barrel torso, small trailing hand, three stepped knuckle plates.

### Luna — Liaison / Kaleidoscope — Mage

- Gameplay identity: fires a thin refracted moonlight ray through every enemy in a line.
- Art-primary trait: **Liaison**, expressed as a body designed around bridging two separated visual masses and maintaining a tense connecting line.
- Secondary channel: **Kaleidoscope** occupies muted refracted panels in fabric and skin, with disciplined color separation rather than rainbow crystal.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** tall mediator with two counterweighted shoulder veils linked across the hands; **B** narrow duelist whose split train aligns into one beam; **C** wide triangular robe cut by a single empty horizontal channel. **Selected: B**, preserving an attractive magical lane while making the line-cast and predatory intent unmistakable.
- Survival psychology: Luna survives by making herself indispensable between enemies, then controlling what each side is allowed to perceive.
- Villain read: seductive triangulator—calm, observant, and delighted by mutual dependence.
- Primary prop/anatomy: no carried staff; one split refractor train runs from forearms to heels and snaps into an aiming line.
- Supernatural cost: every beam fractures another expression across her face and leaves incompatible eye colors and ages in narrow bands.
- Palette/material: dusty petrol, faded coral, smoke violet, old ivory, tarnished silver; matte woven panels, no gemstone gloss, crescent staff, cleavage armor, leg slit, platforms, or orbiting moons.
- 96 px survival: tall narrow body, split colored train, one razor-straight horizontal casting line.

### Malachor — Titan / Fortified / Sanguine — Tank

- Gameplay identity: pins a priority enemy, hardens through incoming damage, heals himself, and leaves a flesh-debt clock.
- Art-primary trait: **Sanguine**, expressed as predatory debt embodied in living tissue rather than vampire fashion.
- Secondary channels: **Fortified** is the buttressed spine and interlocked skin masonry; **Titan** is colossal load-bearing volume and ground occupation.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** colossal flesh keep with a central pinning arm; **B** many-backed crawler under one tower torso; **C** square standing mass whose rib-buttresses close like a trap. **Selected: C**, a non-armored fortress silhouette distinct from Veyra's cocoon and Korath's burden.
- Survival psychology: Malachor survives by making other bodies pay the maintenance cost of his own impossible continuity.
- Villain read: panicked entitlement inside a slow, impersonal siege engine.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one enormous pinning hand shaped like a mortised clamp; no shield or weapon.
- Supernatural cost: healing steals tissue from embedded debtor faces while a visible flesh clock tightens around the sternum.
- Palette/material: dried oxblood, smoked sinew, corpse-ivory cartilage, charcoal scab, rusted surgical staples; no black plate, green cracks, horns, or gothic golem armor.
- 96 px survival: square colossal torso, clamp hand, rib-buttress corners, circular chest clock.

### Sable — Vindicator / Scholar — Marksman

- Gameplay identity: fires a line shot that repeatedly shreds defenses through an on-hit scholarly mark.
- Art-primary trait: **Vindicator**, expressed as a narrow aiming wedge and a body committed to one evidentiary line.
- Secondary channel: **Scholar** appears in ballistic proof-strips laminated into the weapon and sleeve, not floating pages.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** long leaning rifle triangle with rear counterweight; **B** upright witness-stand body around a vertical firearm; **C** prone mantis-like shooter with split elbows. **Selected: A**, preserving the scholarly long-rifle hook while escaping the wide hat and black cloak.
- Survival psychology: Sable survives doubt by turning every grievance into evidence and every person into a target that can confirm it.
- Villain read: cold self-righteous certainty, never theatrical rage.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one extremely long footnote rifle with proof strips laminated along the barrel.
- Supernatural cost: each shot erases words from memory and burns a matching citation scar across the aiming eye.
- Palette/material: faded slate linen, gunmetal, dirty paper, dried burgundy wax, pale scar tissue; no trench cloak, wide-brim hat, mask-as-face-substitute, floating talismans, or secondary sidearm.
- 96 px survival: long diagonal rifle, forward wedge posture, one pale eye scar, short rectangular rear counterweight.
