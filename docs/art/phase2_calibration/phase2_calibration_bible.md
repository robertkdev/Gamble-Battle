## Purpose and acceptance envelope

This packet stress-tests the Phase 1 trait system on twelve real roster units. It is concept art only: one rough silhouette triptych and one definitive full-body master concept per unit. Review derivatives are limited to one crop-and-scale face/head enlargement and one deterministic 96 px board-read check from that same master. No independently reinterpreted headshots, shop cards, sprite sheets, animation sheets, or VFX assets are in scope. The authoritative ten-field psychology and conditional-gate records are `phase2_unit_psychology_records.json`.

The twelve units jointly cover all 22 traits, every combat role, two-trait and three-trait combinations, human/monstrous/alien body plans, peak-condition adult gender/body lanes, and attractive/grotesque/feral/nonhuman lanes. Age outside the peak-condition rule is allowed only through an explicit deliberate-horror exception. Existing gameplay identity and the strongest recognizable hook survive; the calibration intentionally does not preserve weak costumes.

Global gates:

- Every character reads as a villain or dangerous antagonist, but villain emotion varies: shame, administrative contempt, acquisitive calculation, predatory opportunism, feral vacancy, cold instruction, labor rage, manipulative intimacy, or bodily panic.
- Every human or humanlike fighter reads at peak fighting age and condition. Age displacement is allowed only when the design is deliberately horrific and horror/nonhuman anatomy reads before age; Nyxa is the only female deliberate-horror exception in this calibration group.
- Applicable female designs read as hot adult women with lean, toned, feminine builds, never heavy/powerful, overly buff, masculine, juvenile, or elderly. Female frontliners require role-justified armor or nonhuman protective skin; armor is never forced on a unit whose role and survival logic do not justify it.
- The face is a psychology surface, not a beauty insert. Every unit must specify and visibly attempt all ten fields in `phase2_unit_psychology_records.json`: core wound, private want, primary fear, survival strategy, villainous distortion, current emotion, emotional contradiction, face direction, pose direction, and forbidden read. Nonhuman units must provide an equivalent facial mechanism.
- Dry tactical-gothic finish: dusty matte cloth, smoked bone, oxidized metal, bruised flesh, soot, wax, old paper, and mineral surfaces. No porcelain skin, glossy gacha polish, clean-fantasy glamour, or decorative particle halos.
- One dominant prop or anatomical idea per unit. Secondary traits occupy separate channels and may not add a second competing prop.
- Magic must originate from a body, garment, structure, or chosen prop. Floating effects cannot carry trait identity.
- Board read is judged from the unchanged master at 96 px. Tiny details may reward close view but cannot rescue silhouette, role, or identity.
- Neighbor collision check is against the other eleven calibration units plus the retained Vellum/Paisley/Creep/Morwen world anchors. No shared black-coat wardrobe, identical hourglass bodies, repeated horns, repeated wings, repeated orbiting objects, or one universal angry face.

## Unit records

### Korath — Titan / Blessed — Tank

- Gameplay identity: diverts nearby allies' damage into himself, stores pressure, then dashes, stuns, heals an ally, and releases the punishment.
- Art-primary trait: **Blessed**, carried by bowed posture, ruined devotional anatomy, and compulsory penance.
- Secondary channel: **Titan** lives only in immense load-bearing mass, feet, shoulders, and ground pressure; it adds no heroic armor.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** broken processional arch carried across the shoulders; **B** kneeling reliquary hunch with forearms planted; **C** bell-shaped stone penitent with split mantle. **Selected: A**, because the lowered head under a crushing horizontal burden reads disgrace before angel and tank before saint.
- Survival psychology: Korath protects others because every wound he steals proves that the failed miracle still needs him; he cannot distinguish service from self-punishment.
- Villain read: coercive martyr who makes allies indebted to his suffering.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one fused penitential yoke/reliquary beam, grown through both shoulders.
- Supernatural cost: redirected wounds calcify as pale stone blooms, goldless stigmata fissures, and collapsed sensation; his face remains visible, exhausted, and ashamed.
- Palette/material: ash limestone, bruised umber flesh, old linen, smoked bone, minute dead-ochre residue. No bright gold, white wings, halo disc, clean plate, or radiant backlight.
- 96 px survival: bowed head trapped below the long shoulder beam, giant planted feet, one bright cracked chest wound.
- Blessed gate: fallen/disgraced first; shame visible in face and posture; miracle cost physical; no holy hero, paladin, black-wing demon, or pretty sad angel.

### Veyra — Aegis / Bulwark — Tank

- Gameplay identity: converts Aegis stacks into short flat damage reduction, then permanent combat max-health gain.
- Art-primary trait: **Aegis**, expressed as nested living enclosures folding around a vulnerable center.
- Secondary channel: **Bulwark** appears in compressed square buttresses and locking joints, never a carried shield.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** tall sealed shard-shell on two short pillar legs; **B** low multi-legged opened bastion; **C** blunt vertical egg/cocoon with one off-center crack exposing a bound larval core. **Selected: C**, restoring the user's unique closed-egg identity rather than forcing a creature face or generic active clamp.
- Survival psychology: Veyra survives by sealing every new injury behind another layer until protection becomes imprisonment.
- Villain read: possessive protector that treats allies as contents, not people.
- Primary prop/anatomy: no prop; nested shell plates, one off-center crack, and the bound worm-like core form one sealed living egg around chosen dependents.
- Supernatural cost: each hardening permanently fuses one joint and deadens a sensory slit.
- Palette/material: chalky ceramic shell without shine, blue-gray tendon, oxidized copper seams, dim plum core.
- 96 px survival: blunt closed egg/cocoon, two short pillar legs, one central crack, and the exposed bound larval core.

### Cashmere — Arcanist / Mogul — Mage

- Gameplay identity: fires an arcane blast; a kill can pay out gold.
- Art-primary trait: **Mogul**, expressed through claimed distance, the ledger carried as owned capital at the outer hip, and proprietary contract-casting posture.
- Secondary channel: **Arcanist** lives in measured diagram seams and one controlled casting line through the bare inked hand.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** tall asymmetric ledger mantle with exposed casting arm; **B** fitted layered accounting vest and split drape around one huge ledger; **C** split-column garment with severe diagonal ledger block. **Selected: B**, preserving elegant authority while correcting the former elderly broad/soft body lane.
- Survival-psychology record:
  1. **Core wound:** the first time a paid kill erased a beloved person's face from her memory, she discovered that profit could make an intimate loss feel orderly.
  2. **Private want:** to own enough people, knowledge, and possible outcomes that nothing important can ever disappear beyond retrieval again.
  3. **Primary fear:** an unpriced uncertainty will expose that she no longer knows what she sacrificed, whom she loved, or why any acquisition matters.
  4. **Survival strategy:** price, classify, contract, and own every danger before it can surprise or abandon her.
  5. **Villainous distortion:** she treats people as assets and spends their lives, choices, and memories as expendable capital.
  6. **Current emotional state:** pleasurable certainty while closing an acquisition she believes is already hers.
  7. **Emotional contradiction:** inviting proprietary warmth over tightly concealed panic about the blank memory she cannot price.
  8. **Face direction:** one eye sharply appraises the viewer while the other is fractionally unfocused; a tiny asymmetrical brow pinch and tight jaw betray the fear beneath a small proprietary invitation at one mouth corner.
  9. **Pose direction:** the large ledger is clamped under one outer arm at hip height without hiding her torso; the opposite ink-dark hand reaches into the viewer's space in a precise contract cast, and her planted stance implies the acquisition is already closed.
  10. **Forbidden read:** no generic aristocratic smirk, neutral beauty model, friendly merchant, angry executive, sad sorceress, or merely pretty occult accountant.
- Villain read: warm, patient acquisition; she offers ownership as safety while spending other people as entries.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one cracked arcane ledger held vertically like a legal shield.
- Supernatural cost: every paid kill erases a personal memory and prints it as black tax-script through her fingers and throat.
- Body lane: attractive 30-32-year-old adult woman in peak fighting condition; lean, toned, feminine, and unarmored.
- Palette/material: dusty tobacco velvet with soft nap, fibrous worn parchment linen, bruised mauve, dull verdigris brass, cracked old paper and dry wood, scuffed matte leather, and sooty-waxy ink-blackened skin. No wet skin, polished-leather uniforming, dense all-over embossing, elderly or broad/soft coding, generic black trench coat, floating coins, spectacles-as-entire-identity, or orb.
- 96 px survival: narrow vertical authority silhouette, rectangular ledger block, one raised ink-dark casting arm.

### Nyxa — Sanguine / Chronomancer — Marksman

- Gameplay identity: turns four attacks into chaotic multi-arrow volleys and adds another arrow on each cast.
- Art-primary trait: **Chronomancer**, expressed by one body at incompatible ages along a controlled long-range axis.
- Secondary channel: **Sanguine** lives in predatory apertures, clotted string, and blood exchanged for each additional arrow.
- Three silhouette possibilities: **A** temporally aged batlike predator compressed around a crescent spine-bow; **B** four-armed narrow tower whose lower arms age backward; **C** wingless membrane cloak stretched into a bow. **Selected: A**, because frightening nonhuman predation reads before age while preserving Nyxa's feral ancestry and rejecting generic horned gothic anatomy.
- Survival psychology: Nyxa hoards possible futures as ammunition because choosing one life would mean admitting the others are dead.
- Villain read: ecstatic predator who experiences victims as rehearsal timelines.
- Primary prop/anatomy: one asymmetrical time-bow grown from a calcified wing finger and spine.
- Supernatural cost: every extra arrow ages one side of her face and drains blood backward into older scars.
- Palette/material: dried carmine membrane, soot-black fur, nicotine bone, cold lilac age bruising; no skull trinkets, horns, tail clutter, or glossy demon plate.
- 96 px survival: long diagonal bow, folded batlike hunch, one pale ancient half-face.
- Conditional gate: Nyxa is the calibration group's only female deliberate-horror exception. The hot-adult-woman and lean/toned/feminine gates are not applicable only because the current image reads batlike temporal predator before old woman; ordinary crone, fragile elder, or age-as-the-only-horror remains forbidden.
